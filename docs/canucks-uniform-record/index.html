No, the Canucks don't play better because of their skate jerseys

How favorable matchups create the illusion of uniforms impacting team performance.

By Miles Bolton

In January, Daily Hive Vancouver reported that the Canucks win more when they wear their alternate uniforms â€” their vintage black, yellow and red "skate" set.

This was technically true. At the time, the Canucks were 9-1-0 in their skate uniform and 8-3-2 in their regular home set. By the end of the regular season, the Canucks averaged more goals per game and had a higher winning percentage in games they wore their alternate uniforms.

What Daily Hive failed to account for in this article was which games the team picked to wear their throwbacks. This season, they were generally worn against weaker opponents, allowing the Canucks to score more goals. The home uniforms, conversely, were often worn for games against better teams. The road uniforms were worn for each of Vancouver's 41 away games and were not worn at home.
